User Research

For the research, I conducted interviews with people who fit our target audience. The target audience were people who: travel often, use third-party travel apps to book flights, are in their 20s - mid 30s, and have busy schedules. The interviews consisted of 10 - 13 questions about their flight booking habits and other experiences they’ve had with online booking services and apps.

Background

Booking flights is usually an arduous process for most. Even with all the tools that are currently out there to help alleviate the frustrations associated with booking said flights, it can still be a daunting process that ultimately leaves users unhappy with their purchase. I set out to find out what users look for when booking flights, from the moment they get to their assigned gate to the moment they get off the plane.

Interview Plan

  1. Do users prioritize price over experience?

  2. What information is most important to the user when evaluating flights?

Biggest Finding

Users prioritize the use of points from loyalty programs. This sways their booking decisions a lot of the time.

Definition & Ideation

Utilizing the insights gained from the research phase, I focused on defining the main problem that the app would be solving as well as ideating the ways it would go about solving the problem.

Problem Statement

Emily who is a young professional with limited paid-time-off and money needs a service that can help her track her accumulated loyalty points because she wants to take advantage of these rewards and make sure she’s using them to get the best possible value. How might we help these young professionals with budgetary constraints keep track of the loyalty points they earn when traveling while maximizing the value they get from these points?

Main Takeaway

Current competitors are either too complex or not focusing on travel at all. This gives us a clear niche to position our app in as a simple points tracking solution focused on travel points and miles, with an aesthetically pleasing UI that adheres to Apple’s HIG standards.

Onboarding User Flow

A lot of the competitors I analyzed had very cumbersome onboarding processes. As a user, you often don’t want to commit to signing up for an app or service before trying it. This made it a priority for the onboarding to be as simple as possible and having the Sign Up be completely optional.

Testing & Iteration

After working on the fully clickable protoype, I moved on to do usability testing to help me discover and fix any pain points that arose. The testing was done through Zoom while the user shared their screen so that I could observe their actions. I also encouraged the user to think aloud, that way I could understand the thought process behind their choices when testing.

Guerrila Testing Observations

  • User skipped the Sign Up process without noticing.
  • Edit button on Points Details is not clear.
  • The Points History needs to be clearer.
  • Visual feedback is needed to confirm points were added.
  • It makes more sense to take the user to the home screen after adding points.

The simplicity of the app makes it hard for the user to understand what they are looking at in some screens like the Points Details. As such, it could use with some better labeling and direction.
