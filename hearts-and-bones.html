My Role

User Research

In order to gain useful insights of our target users, I conducted user interviews over Zoom. The people interviewed were people who either had adopted from a pet rescue in the past or had considered doing so recently. Additionally, I launched a survey to allow us to reach more people.

Primary Research Goal

Understand the needs of people who are looking to adopt dogs and how the website can help them achieve that goal.

Secondary Research Questions

  1. Why or why not they have chosen to adopt online

  2. Why and how are they choosing to adopt from their chosen rescue

  3. What questions did users have during the adoption process

  4. In an ideal world, what would the adoption process look like

of users surveyed started their adoption search online.

Definition & Ideation

After the research phase, I had a good idea of the pain points users were currently having with other solutions. I had also identified things that could be improved on the current website design such as better navigation and overall layout of information.

Bio

Rebecca is a 29 year old Graphic Designer living in New York City. She lives alone and generally works from home. She loves animals and longs to find the perfect companion during those long days at home.

Goals

  • Wants to see testimonials of the rescue she's considering.
  • Wants to know what the adoption process will be like, step-by-step, before starting the journey.
  • Wants to see multiple pictures, and even videos, of the dog she's considering adopting.

Frustrations

  • Not sure what exact breed and size to adopt
  • Up-to-date shelter list of adoptable pets.
  • Really long adoption forms with no guarantee of adoption.
  • Transparency regarding behavior and health issues.

Behaviors

  • Animal Lover, lives alone, wants pet companion.
  • Outgoing and extroverted, enjoys social interactions, such as spending time at dog parks and socializing with other pet owners.
  • Prefers to have structure in their lives, with plans and routines firmly in place.
  • Fosters animals from shelter home.

Problem Statement

Rebecca, a young busy professional who lives alone, wants to adopt a dog but has some apprehension about applying online because they want to put their limited time and effort into applying for a dog from a trustworthy and reliable rescue. How might we facilitate the adoption process in a way that also builds trust in the rescue?

Description

Online adoption can be a daunting process for most people. They need to be able to trust that the rescue they are using is reliable, ethical and not overly complicated.

Impact

A solution for this problem could have a positive impact on people looking to adopt by making it easier to find a companion to be there for them when they need it most.

Testing & Iteration

After working on the fully clickable protoype, I moved on to do usability testing to help me discover and fix any pain points that arose. The testing was done through Zoom while the user shared their screen so that I could observe their actions. I also encouraged the user to think aloud, that way I could understand the thought process behind their choices when testing.

Guerrila Testing Observations

  • User skipped the Sign Up process without noticing.
  • Edit button on Points Details is not clear.
  • The Points History needs to be clearer.
  • Visual feedback is needed to confirm points were added.
  • It makes more sense to take the user to the home screen after adding points.

The simplicity of the app makes it hard for the user to understand what they are looking at in some screens like the Points Details. As such, it could use with some better labeling and direction.
